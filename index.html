My Meeting with Three Minds

   

I set out to understand code — and met three concepts that define the future of technology.

 
 
   

The First Mind: Artificial Intelligence (AI)

       

This was the broadest mind, the parent concept. It spoke of systems that can "think and act" rationally.       It was the grand       idea of simulating human intellect.

 
 
   

The Second Mind: Machine Learning (ML)

       

This one didn't speak in rules, but in patterns. It consumed vast amounts of data, learning and adapting without being explicitly programmed for every task.

 
 
   

The Third Mind: Deep Learning (DL)

       

The final mind was complex, layered like a human brain. It processed information through intricate neural networks, solving problems the others found impossible.
